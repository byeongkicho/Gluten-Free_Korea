## Design Context

### Users
- Primary: People with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity living in or visiting Korea
- Secondary: Health-conscious individuals exploring gluten-free dining options
- Context: Mobile-first — users often check the site while out, deciding where to eat. Need quick scanning, clear information hierarchy
- Job to be done: Find a trustworthy gluten-free restaurant/cafe/bakery nearby, quickly understand what to expect, and navigate there

### Brand Personality
- **Trustworthy** — This is health-critical information. The site must feel reliable, not gimmicky
- **Warm & approachable** — Not clinical or sterile. Eating out should feel enjoyable, not stressful
- **Handcrafted** — Should feel like a thoughtful local guide made by someone who cares, not an AI-generated directory

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Clean, editorial, organic. Think a well-designed food magazine or a curated local guide zine
- **NOT**: Generic SaaS dashboard, AI-generated template, corporate directory
- **Anti-references**: Cookie-cutter Tailwind templates, generic card grids, emerald/green pill buttons everywhere
- **Theme**: Light mode primary, dark mode supported. Warm neutrals (not pure gray), earthy accent colors
- **Typography**: Should feel editorial — distinctive headings, comfortable body text. Not Inter/system defaults
- **Color palette direction**: Warm, earthy tones. Think wheat/grain colors, warm greens (not neon emerald), soft cream backgrounds. The current emerald-heavy palette feels too "tech startup"

### Design Principles
1. **Trust through clarity** — Every piece of information should be immediately scannable. No ambiguity about what a place offers
2. **Warmth over efficiency** — This isn't a productivity tool. Small delightful details matter more than utilitarian density
3. **Content-first** — The places are the stars. UI should frame them, not compete with them
4. **Mobile-native** — Design for the person standing on a Seoul street corner, not sitting at a desk
5. **Honest simplicity** — No decorative complexity. Every element earns its place
